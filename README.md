## Welcome

Welcome to the OpenSearch Project community repository.

The OpenSearch Project is supported by [The OpenSearch Software Foundation](https://foundation.opensearch.org/), a project of [The Linux Foundation](https://www.linuxfoundation.org/). You can read the launch announcement [here](https://www.linuxfoundation.org/press/linux-foundation-announces-opensearch-software-foundation-to-foster-open-collaboration-in-search-and-analytics) and learn more about joining the foundation [here](https://foundation.opensearch.org/). 

The project has a Governing Board and a [Technical Steering Committee](https://github.com/opensearch-project/technical-steering).

### **Blog contributions**
The OpenSearch Project blog reflects the same principles that guide our open source community. Our blog is community-driven and inclusive by design—amplifying diverse voices, welcoming global participation, and creating accessible content for all. Through consistent editorial standards and thoughtful review, we aim to deliver high-quality content that informs, inspires, and strengthens the OpenSearch ecosystem. 
* For guidance on writing blog content, community members should follow the [OpenSearch Blog Guidelines](https://opensearch.org/blog/guidelines/) and use the [OpenSearch Writing Assistant](https://opensearch.org/blog/how-to-write-better-blog-posts-with-the-opensearch-writing-assistant/) to get help with getting started and finding inspiration. *Types of community blogs, not limited to: Tutorials and how-to guides, event recaps and conference summaries, integration and deployment walkthroughs, case studies and real-world experiences, best practices, explorations of new features or experimental workflows, and contributor stories and project insights.*

### OpenSearch Community User Group Program

OpenSearch Community User Groups bring together developers, operators, and enthusiasts to learn, build, and grow around OpenSearch — the open source, enterprise-grade search and observability suite that brings order to unstructured data at scale.

#### Our community meetups are designed with these goals in mind:
* **Promote OpenSearch adoption** by connecting developers, data engineers, and organizations leveraging search, observability, and analytics at scale.
* **Share practical knowledge** through technical talks, live demos, and real-world use cases from people running OpenSearch in production.
* **Support contributors and advocates** by providing a platform to discuss features, plugins, and enhancements that improve the OpenSearch ecosystem.
* **Encourage collaboration** between maintainers and users in an open, transparent, and inclusive environment where feedback drives innovation.
* **Build strong local OpenSearch communities** so users and contributors can network, collaborate, and grow the project globally.

#### OpenSearch User Group Process

1. Request a new user group [here.](https://github.com/opensearch-project/community/issues/new?template=new_user_group.yaml)

2. Planning an OpenSearch Meetup? Review the OpenSearch User Groups Charter and Guidelines [here.](https://github.com/opensearch-project/community/blob/main/user-groups/charter-guidelines.md)

3. Promote your event. If you’re organizing a new OpenSearch User Group and want help promoting, submit your event information through the [OpenSearch Community form under social post](https://form.asana.com/?k=kLbetrjFp3M08BQEMxmAAA&d=9283783873717). 
    Let the OpenSearch project help share your User Groups so it reaches the right audience and strengthens the global OpenSearch Community.

4. After Your User Group, keep the momentum going after your OpenSearch Meetup:
    * Share highlights: Post photos, slides, demo links, or recordings on your personal social media chanels using #OpenSearchCommunity or in [OpenSearch Forum](https://forum.opensearch.org/). This allows those who couldn’t attend to benefit and helps promote the meetup across OpenSearch channels.
    * Gather feedback: Ask attendees what worked well and what could be improved for future events.
