### **Blog Contributions**
The OpenSearch Project blog reflects the same principles that guide our open source community. Our blog is community-driven and inclusive by design—amplifying diverse voices.

**Community blog topics include (but are not limited to):** tutorials and how-to guides, event recaps and conference summaries, integration and deployment walkthroughs, case studies and real-world experiences, best practices, explorations of new features or experimental workflows, and contributor stories and project insights.

### Blog Contributions

The OpenSearch blog is community-driven and open to all contributors. Topics include tutorials, event recaps, deployment walkthroughs, case studies, best practices, feature explorations, and contributor stories.

#### How to Contribute

1. **Find inspiration** — Browse the [Blog Content Calendar + Tracker](https://github.com/orgs/opensearch-project/projects/80/views/3) or use the [OpenSearch Writing Assistant](https://opensearch.org/blog/how-to-write-better-blog-posts-with-the-opensearch-writing-assistant/).
2. **Open an issue** — Use the [blog post issue template](https://github.com/opensearch-project/project-website/issues/new?template=blog_post.yml) on GitHub.
3. **Draft your post** — Follow the [editorial process and content guides](https://opensearch.org/blog/guidelines/).
4. **Submit your draft** — Use the [blog intake form](https://form.asana.com/?k=kLbetrjFp3M08BQEMxmAAA&d=9283783873717) at least **10 working days** before your target publication date.
   > - Content must be finalized before submission.
   > - If co-authoring with another company, get their approval before submitting.
   > - The Linux Foundation does not provide technical review but may involve the Technical Steering Committee if needed.
5. **Review** — The Linux Foundation communications team will respond within **48 hours**.
6. **Publication**
- Posts publish on **Tuesdays and Thursdays** (exceptions for time-sensitive content).
- Timeline: **5–10 working days** from final submission to publication.
- Distribution: OpenSearch website, Linux Foundation properties, and OpenSearch social media.

#### Guidelines
- Write original content with a clear community benefit.
- Keep a professional but approachable tone.
- Write for a global audience — avoid regional slang and unexplained jargon.

### OpenSearch Community User Group Program

OpenSearch Community User Groups are community-driven meetups, or recurring gatherings who discuss OpenSearch topics, share knowledge, present related solutions, and network with the wider open source community.

#### OpenSearch User Group Process

1. Request a new user group [here.](https://github.com/opensearch-project/community/issues/new?template=new_user_group.yaml)

2. Planning an OpenSearch Meetup? Review the OpenSearch User Groups Charter and Guidelines [here.](https://github.com/opensearch-project/community/blob/main/user-groups/charter-guidelines.md)

3. Promote your event. If you’re organizing a new OpenSearch User Group and want help promoting, submit your event information through the [OpenSearch Community form under social post](https://form.asana.com/?k=kLbetrjFp3M08BQEMxmAAA&d=9283783873717). 
    Let the OpenSearch project help share your User Groups so it reaches the right audience and strengthens the global OpenSearch Community.

4. After Your User Group, keep the momentum going after your OpenSearch Meetup:
    * Share highlights: Post photos, slides, demo links, or recordings on your personal social media chanels using #OpenSearchCommunity or in [OpenSearch Forum](https://forum.opensearch.org/). This allows those who couldn’t attend to benefit and helps promote the meetup across OpenSearch channels.
    * Gather feedback: Ask attendees what worked well and what could be improved for future events.
